mini-inet — Stage 1: order flow through a matching engine

Replicating the architecture from Brian Nigito's Jane Street talk "How to Build an Exchange" in Python. A client submits a TCP order to a Port; the Port hands it to the single Matching Engine (in-memory order book on one box); the ME emits a sequenced event that is multicast to every consumer at once — Market Data, Drop, Trade Reporter, and a passive secondary ME. Same stream, deterministic replay, "shoot any consumer in the head" and it rebuilds from seq 0.

What this is showing. The talk's "crazy" single-machine design: one in-memory matching engine, with client-facing Ports doing per-client TCP sequencing and flow control, and an internal UDP multicast bus so that every consumer — the two trade counterparties via their Ports, plus MD, Drop, the Trade Reporter, and the passive secondary ME — sees the same sequenced event at the same moment. Determinism is the whole point: any consumer can crash, replay the stream from a retransmitter, and rebuild identical state. The passive ME listens to the primary's output stream (not raw client UDP) so it deterministically reproduces the same events and can fail over.

Why this scenario. MSCO BUY 500@3329 rests on the book. GSCO sells 500@3329 — the seller would have accepted 3329, and the resting buy was offering 3329, so they cross and trade at the resting price. (Price improvement always goes to the aggressor — Stage 1's price_improvement_to_aggressor test encodes the Instinet cautionary tale.)
